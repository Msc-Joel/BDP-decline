More than half of CEDA loan beneficiaries are in the services business

The services sector, where most people in Botswana work, is the go to venture for new startups who get seed capital from a low-interest government loan facility, according to analysis of the Citizen Entrepreneurial Development Agency (CEDA) businesses.
CEDA is a Botswana government institution that provides development funding to citizen owned businesses through low interest rates. CEDA data shows that out of 20500 entrepreneurs funded since 2005, over 13700 are in the services sector. This is more than two-third of the businesses funded by the agency.
The services sector includes the financial services, the insurance industry and other credit financing organizations. The sector is the largest creator of jobs in the land-locked country struggling to diversify its economy from mining.
The services sector is then followed by the agribusiness sector, which accounts for 25% of the CEDA funded businesses, according to the database. Other sectors contribute just 9% to the CEDA funded business. Other enterprises include tourism, media, manufacturing and commercial property.

The service sector data is important in understanding employment landscape in Botswana. The sector includes which hiring appeared to slow in November, but the unemployment rate tumbled from 4.6% to 4.2%. That is a historically low jobless rate though still above the pre-pandemic level of 3.5%.

Data also shows that majority of CEDA businesses are classified as small business and employ roughly 47% of the private sector workforce and most businesses are located in urban areas, particularly the capital city.
